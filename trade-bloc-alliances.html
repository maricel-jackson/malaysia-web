Welcome to Global Trade Bloc Alliances

Malaysia actively participates in several trade bloc alliances, each aiming to enhance economic cooperation and integration among member countries.

Read More

ASEAN

Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) Established on August 8, 1967, ASEAN is a regional organization comprising ten Southeast Asian countries, including Malaysia. Its primary goal is to promote economic growth, social progress, and cultural development in the region. ASEAN facilitates collaboration and mutual assistance among members, aiming to accelerate economic integration and stability.​

AFTA

ASEAN Free Trade Area (AFTA) Initiated in 1992, AFTA is an agreement among ASEAN countries to reduce or eliminate tariffs on intra-ASEAN trade in goods. The objective is to increase ASEAN's competitive edge as a production base in the global market through the elimination of tariffs and non-tariff barriers among member countries.

RCEP

Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP) Signed on November 15, 2020, RCEP is a free trade agreement among 15 Asia-Pacific nations, including Malaysia. It is the world's largest trade bloc, encompassing approximately 30% of the global population and GDP. RCEP aims to establish a comprehensive and mutually beneficial economic partnership, covering trade in goods and services, investment, economic and technical cooperation, and more. ​

APEC

Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) APEC is a regional economic forum established in 1989, comprising 21 Pacific Rim countries, including Malaysia. Its primary goal is to promote free trade and economic cooperation throughout the Asia-Pacific region. APEC works to reduce tariffs and other trade barriers across the Asia-Pacific region, creating efficient domestic economies and increasing exports.​

D-8

D-8 Organization for Economic Cooperation Also known as Developing-8, the D-8 is an organization for development cooperation among eight developing countries, including Malaysia. Established in 1997, the D-8 aims to improve member states' position in the global economy, diversify and create new opportunities in trade relations, enhance participation in decision-making at the international level, and improve standards of living. ​

WTO

World Trade Organization (WTO) Malaysia is a member of the WTO, an international organization established to supervise and liberalize international trade. The WTO provides a framework for negotiating trade agreements, a dispute resolution process, and aims to ensure that trade flows as smoothly, predictably, and freely as possible.​
